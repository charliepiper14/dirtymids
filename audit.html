CRO Audit: Landing Page Analysis

Based on uploaded screenshots + cognitive bias analysis

Overall Assessment

Your landing pages are solid but not optimized. The brand aesthetic is strong (clean, athletic, aspirational), but you're missing key conversion drivers that could push your 3% conversion rate to 5%+.

What's Working Well

  • Visual identity is on point: Clean product photos, strong brand presence, athletic energy
  • Mobile-friendly layout: Important since 86% of traffic is mobile
  • "Dirty Mids in the Wild" UGC section: Shows real athletes wearing the gear (social proof)
  • Clear product categories: Easy to find Original Dirty Mids, Piped Collection, etc.
  • Free shipping callout: Reduces purchase friction

Critical Issues Hurting Conversion Rate

Issue #1: Weak Hero Section / Value Proposition

Your product pages lead with "ORIGINAL DIRTY MIDS - Baseball Pants" ‚Äî this is too functional. You're not selling pants, you're selling identity and status.

Fix: Replace with identity-driven copy like "The Look Every Real Player Rocks" or "Built for Ballers Who Don't Settle." Your cognitive bias analysis correctly identifies that "Ingroup Identity Bias" is your highest-leverage conversion driver.

Issue #2: Scarcity & Urgency Under-Leveraged

You have a "Restock just went live" banner which is good, but it lacks dynamic reinforcement. There's no stock countdown, no "X people viewing this," no "only Y left in size M."

Fix: Implement real-time urgency indicators. Add a countdown timer for restocks. Show live inventory levels for popular sizes. Test "üî• 12 people have this in their cart right now" social proof.

Issue #3: Loss Aversion is Missing

You're not telling customers what they LOSE by not buying. Fear of missing out and regret avoidance are powerful motivators for your demographic.

Fix: Add messaging like "Don't get caught in baggy pants again" or "Once these restocks sell out, they're gone." Frame the decision as avoiding a negative outcome, not just gaining a positive one.

Issue #4: No Micro-Commitment Funnel

Visitors who aren't ready to buy immediately just bounce. There's no way to engage them.

Fix: Add a "Find Your Perfect Mids" quiz (3-4 questions about style preference, inseam length, position played). This creates psychological ownership and guides hesitant shoppers. People who complete quizzes convert at 3-5x the rate of those who don't.

Issue #5: Social Proof is Present but Weak

You show "Featured Dirty Mids Athletes" which is good, but it lacks quantitative validation. "Worn by 10,000+ players" would be more compelling.

Fix: Add customer count, review ratings prominently on product pages. Include testimonials with photos. Add a "4.9‚òÖ from 2,847 reviews" badge near the Add to Cart button.

Issue #6: No Sticky Add-to-Cart (Mobile)

On mobile, when users scroll down to see product details, the "Add to Bag" button scrolls out of view. This creates unnecessary friction.

Fix: Implement a sticky "Add to Bag" bar that follows the user as they scroll on mobile. This is standard practice for mobile-first e-commerce and can increase mobile conversions by 10-15%.
